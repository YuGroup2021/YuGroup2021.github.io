We have positions for researchers at different levels, including Postdoc, PhD, Master, undergraduate, and visiting student/scholar

  • Postdoc position: Candidates with backgrounds mentioned below (particularly in biosensors, bioelectronics, tissue engineering, soft matter synthesis, and mechanics) are welcome to inquire about the position by contacting Dr. Yu directly.
  • Ph.D. position: We always welcome talented, self-motivated, and hard-working PhD students to join our group. Our group accepts students from the following departments: Electrical and Computer Engineering, Bioengineering, Materials Science and Engineering, and Mechanical Science and Engineering. Please feel free to inquire directly for the most suitable program. Current PhD students or those joining UIUC are encouraged to inquire about positions. Prospective students or candidates with the mentioned backgrounds are welcome to inquire about potential positions. In your applications, please apply to our PhD program and mention your interest in this group.
  • Master student position: Master students who are currently at or will join UIUC are encouraged to inquire about available positions.
  • Undergraduate researcher positions: Our group has positions for undergraduate researchers to engage in frontier interdisciplinary research, enhancing their future graduate school applications and industry career preparations.
  • Visiting Students/Scholars: We are open to hosting visiting students and scholars with similar engineering and science backgrounds.

    • The research projects are highly interdisciplinary. Candidates with prior educational training and research experience in at least one of the following areas will be prioritized:

  • Electronics and Electronic materials: Microelectronics, optoelectronics, solid-state electronics (TFTs, detectors), sensors, device physics
  • Biomedical Engineering: Tissue engineering, electrophysiology, bio-integrated electronics, biosensors, wearable electronics, implantable devices
  • Materials and Chemistry: Organic electronics, semiconductors, inorganic electronics, polymer synthesis
  • Mechanical Engineering: Robotics, soft robotics, human-machine interfaces, advanced manufacturing
  • Solid mechanics: Mechanics of thin films and soft matter
